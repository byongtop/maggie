## The thing that matters more than any of this

Posing is a tool, not a destination. The best photos I take aren't the ones where someone has executed a pose correctly — they're the ones where someone has relaxed enough that the pose stops mattering.

Everything above is useful for removing the specific problems that make photos read as stiff or uncomfortable. But the goal isn't to look like you're posing well. It's to look like yourself in a context where a camera happened to be present.

That takes a bit of time on shoot day. The first twenty minutes are usually the warmup. By the time we're into the session properly, most people have stopped thinking about their hands entirely — which is exactly when the good photos happen.
