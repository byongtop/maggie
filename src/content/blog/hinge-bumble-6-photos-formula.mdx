## The mistakes that waste your slots

A few patterns I see constantly that quietly undermine otherwise decent profiles:

Two photos from the same angle taken on the same day. They look like duplicates even if they're technically different shots. The viewer gets no new information from the second one — it just takes up a slot.

A group photo where you're one of four people and the caption doesn't clarify which one. This happens more than you'd think, and it creates a specific kind of frustration in the person viewing it.

A photo where the background is doing more work than you are. A beautiful Sydney sunset with a small figure in front of it. An interesting wall with someone standing in front of it looking slightly lost. The background should support the photo, not compete with it.

And photos taken at the same time, in the same light, wearing the same clothes — presented as if they're from different occasions. A profile that looks like it was assembled from one afternoon's worth of photos reads as a profile that was assembled from one afternoon's worth of photos.

## The formula in one sentence

Lead with a face, follow with a body, show that you do things, show range, show that other people like you, and end with something that makes meeting you feel like an obvious next step.

Six slots. Six jobs. If each photo is doing something the others aren't, you have a complete profile. If three of them are doing the same thing, you have a profile with three wasted opportunities.
