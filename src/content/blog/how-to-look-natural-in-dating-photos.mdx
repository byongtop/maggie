## The short version

Looking natural in photos is not a talent. It's a set of conditions — movement, genuine distraction, time to warm up, small physical adjustments that change how the body reads on camera.

Most people have never been given the actual information about what creates that ease. They've just been told to smile and relax, which is the least useful instruction a photographer can give.

If you're preparing for a session, the most useful thing you can do is accept that the first twenty minutes will feel slightly awkward and that's completely fine. It's not a sign that something is wrong — it's just the camera warm-up. The photos that don't look like you're being photographed almost always come after it.
