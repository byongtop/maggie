## Colour is a tool, not a rule

The colour guide above is a starting point, not a checklist. I've seen people look incredible in colours that break every one of these suggestions — because the light was right, the location was right, and they felt genuinely comfortable in what they were wearing.

That last part matters more than any colour theory. Confidence photographs. Someone who feels slightly wrong in their outfit — too formal, too casual, not quite themselves — shows it in their posture, their eyes, the way they hold their shoulders. No amount of correct colour choice fixes that.

So use this as a filter, not a rulebook. Get rid of the colours that cause problems, lean towards what works in Sydney's light, and then wear something you actually like.
